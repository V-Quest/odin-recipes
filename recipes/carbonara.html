Carbonara

Description

Carbonara is a delicious classic Roman pasta dish that emphasizes the quality of ingredients used. A mouthwatering creamy sauce is made by emulsifying rendered fat from the guanciale, pasta water, pecorino cheese, and egg. It is truly an Italian dish that one should make in their lifetime.

Substitutions can be made based on availability of ingridients, however for the true taste of pasta carbonara you should attempt to source the classic ingredients. The recipe below is for four people, scale the recipe up or down based on your needs

Ingredients

  • Dried Spaghetti: 16oz
  • Guanciale: 4oz
    • Pancetta or bacon can be substituted
  • Egg:
    • 6 yolk
    • 2 whole
  • Salt: to taste
  • Pepper: to taste
  • Pecorino romano: 4oz grated

Steps

  1. Bring a pot of salted water to a boil, cook spaghetti stirring ocasionally until is cooked two minutes prior to al dente. Reserve some of the pasta water after cooking
  2. Place a medium skillet on low heat, add guanciale sliced into 1cm length and 0.5cm width strips. Mix ocassionally to ensure that it's cooked evenly until crispy brown but not burnt, about 5-10 minutes
  3. Add grated pecorino romano to a bowl, followed by egg yolk, egg, and cracked black pepper. Whisk together until the texture is a thick paste.
  4. Remove guanciale from skillet but keep the rendered fat, add the strained spaghetti and reserved pasta water to skillet and turn on high, cook while stirring vigorously for 2-3 minutes or until pasta is al dente. Remove the skillet from heat and add in the pecorino egg mixture. Stir everything together and add back in the strips of guanciale.
